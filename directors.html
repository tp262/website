Quentin Tarantino

Tarantino is a master of dialogue, and known for his over usage of blood and gore in his movies. Tarantino's movies are all case studies on how to create villains that create actual conflict and tension in the film, and his amazing set pieces that show he can bring you from the Wild West to 1940s World War II with a snap of his fingers.

Joel and Ethan Cohen

The Cohen Brothers are strange anomalies in the movie industry, in where they can create a cult-classic stoner movie and also win best picture with No Country for Old Men in a short time period. Their usage of dialogue and how they film their movies where the camera is always in the middle of the conversation gives the audience an intimate feeling with the characters. Movies like Fargo and Big Lebowski subvert your expectations for the genres they are in, by giving you an experience where serious manners are treated flippantly and meaningless bowling matches are treated with a drawn gun and swears.

Christopher Nolan

It is no exaggeration to say anything Nolan touches truly turns into gold, a director akin to Midas. You attach his name to anything and people will watch it, even if it is paint drying. His cinematic epics that take you across space and time itself while grounding itself in reality somehow are nothing short of breathtaking. Even in an era where superheroes were seen as goofy thanks in part of George Clooney's rendition of Batman, he managed to bring an entire genre back from the brink.
