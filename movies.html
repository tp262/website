Your Name

Directed By Makoto Shinkai

Your Name otherwise known as in Japanese as 君の名は。(Kimi no Na wa) is an animated film written and directed by Makoto Shinkai that beautifully captures that feeling of longing but you cannot explain why. With beautiful animation and an amazing score by the RADWIMPS, Shinkai explores a body swapping scenario between a city boy and country girl both longing for each other's lifestyle only to subvert expectations and take you on a beautiful journey from start to finish.

Get Out

Directed By Jordan Peele

Get Out by Jordan Peele is a genre bending experience that explores the topic of race through the perspective of a horror movie. But Peele's extensive experience in skit comedy on his popular show Key and Peele absolutely shines, and his masterful conducting of pace and tone leaves you immersed and invested the whole time.

Parasite

Directed By Bong Joon-Ho

Parasite, the winner of best film of the year 2020, is a South Korean black comedy thriller film by Bong Joon-ho that was my personal best film of the year as well. It delves into topics such as the wealth gap, class conflicts, and social inequality. Topics that are universal, and not only a statement in South Korea, but in the US as well. Joon-Ho juxtaposes 2 different families and an easy-going sleazy family befriends a wealthy families home in order to all be employed by them and benefit from their wealth without their knowledge but halfway through the film takes a turn that you have to go in blind to experience.

Mad Max: Fury Road

Directed By George Miller

Mad Max: Fury Road is somewhat of a reboot of the original trilogy starring Mel Gibson in the 1980s, with Tom Hardy taking the mantle of the titular character. Fury Road is another chapter in Max's story, a ghost haunted by his past changing the lives of everyone he crosses paths with, known as the Road warrior. Miller's love for practical effects and show not tell story telling that led to amazing world building rivaling even Star Wars, Fury Road is my absolute favorite action film.

Baby Driver

Directed By Edgar Wright

Edgar Wright's Baby Driver is nothing short of another example why he leads the industry in making every scene engaging and having 0 dull moments. A story of Baby, a heist driver wanting to turn away from the life he has led because of love, it follows a boy that was in the wrong place at the wrong time but is damn good at driving. He makes even Baby getting coffee an absolute blast to watch and an example of well paced and fun cinema. An example of what Fast and Furious could be given the right direction.

The Big Lebowski

Directed By The Cohen Brothers

The Big Lebowski by the Cohen Brothers is what I would call an anti-movie. Almost like the Seinfeld of cinema where nothing really happens but it somehow drags you along a strange mystery led by the most unqualified group of people imaginable that unravels but is somehow only secondary to the primary plot of Lebowski just trying to bowl and smoke weed. The way the Cohen Brothers approach dialogue and story telling that is nonchalant in its delivery makes for a weird but extremely funny experience.
